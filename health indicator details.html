Maternal Mortality Rate (Per 100,000 Live Births)

Definition: Maternal mortality rate is the annual number of female deaths resulting from any cause related to pregnancy or its management, or aggravated by them (excluding accidents or incidental causes), occurring during pregnancy, childbirth, or within 42 days of the end of the pregnancy, regardless of the pregnancy period or location, per 100,000 live births in a specified year.

Distribution: Citizenship, Age, Urban/Rural

Data Source: Civil Registry, Population Census, Household Surveys

Immunization Coverage Rate by Vaccine, for Each Vaccine Listed in the National Schedule. SDG Indicator 3.b.1

Definition: Percentage of targeted population who have received the last recommended dose of each vaccine listed in the national immunization schedule, by vaccine. This should include all vaccines listed in the country’s routine immunization schedule (such as BCG vaccine for tuberculosis; polio; conjugated pneumococcal vaccine; rotavirus; pentavalent vaccine for diphtheria, tetanus, whooping cough, Haemophilus influenza type b, and hepatitis B; measles vaccine; rubella; human papillomavirus vaccine; tetanus; influenza; and other vaccines as defined by the national schedule) (WHO).

Data Source: Household surveys

War-Related Deaths Per 100,000 Population. SDG Indicator 16.1.2

Definition:In a narrow sense, war-related deaths are those directly caused by combat such as in traditional battlefield fighting and bombing operations. In a broader sense, they include killings that constitute war crimes, such as targeting civilians or military personnel outside of combat zones. Rates are defined as the total number of intentional killings and war-related deaths, divided by the total population, expressed per 100,000 population (OHCHR).

Distribution: Nationality, gender, age, urban/rural, cause

Data Source: Estimates
